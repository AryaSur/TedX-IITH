TEDx is a program that allows individuals or groups to independently organize and host their own local TED-like events, known as TEDx events. The "x" in TEDx stands for "independently organized event." These events follow the general format and spirit of TED (Technology, Entertainment, Design) conferences but are organized at a local level and are not directly affiliated with the main TED organization.

TEDx events are designed to provide a platform for local communities to share ideas, inspire, and spark meaningful conversations. They bring together a diverse range of speakers from various fields such as science, technology, art, business, and more. The talks at TEDx events are typically limited to a maximum of 18 minutes and aim to communicate ideas worth spreading.

TEDx events have grown in popularity worldwide since their inception in 2009. They provide a unique opportunity for individuals to share their knowledge, experiences, and innovative ideas on a local stage and contribute to the global TED community. These events are often live-streamed and recorded, allowing the talks to reach a wider audience beyond the physical event.
